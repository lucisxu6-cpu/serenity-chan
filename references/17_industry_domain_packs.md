# Industry Domain Packs

Use these packs before theme scans and strategy forecasts. They define the industry-specific questions AI must answer after deterministic data arrives.

## Robotics

Value-chain layers:

- Reducer and precision transmission.
- Servo, frameless torque motor, hollow-cup motor, driver, and encoder.
- Controller, machine vision, sensors, and motion-control software.
- Body integrator and application integrator.
- End-user deployment, factory verification, and maintenance network.

Evidence to seek:

- Product revenue split and margin by component.
- Customer validation, order backlog, framework agreement, or production line delivery.
- Capacity expansion, yield, import substitution evidence, and price erosion.
- Relationship between robot demand and issuer revenue.

Common downgrade patterns:

- Pure concept exposure without product revenue.
- Body integrator with weak margin capture.
- Upstream component claim without customer or volume evidence.

## AI Compute And High-Speed Interconnect

Value-chain layers:

- Optical modules, optical chips, lasers, connectors, and CPO.
- PCB, HDI, high-speed copper, thermal, power, and structural parts.
- Server ODM, switch, NIC, accelerator, and rack-scale integration.
- Data-center power, cooling, and deployment operations.
- Cloud, model, and application demand that funds capex.

Evidence to seek:

- 800G / 1.6T shipment evidence, overseas customer mix, and gross-margin durability.
- AI server or switch revenue split, backlog, and customer concentration.
- Capacity expansion timing, capex, inventory, and receivables.
- Capex cycle durability from hyperscalers and domestic AI infrastructure.

Common downgrade patterns:

- AI label with generic electronics revenue.
- Valuation already pricing H4/H5 while disclosures only support H2/H3.
- Single customer dependence without visible order durability.

## Innovative Medicine And Biotech

Value-chain layers:

- Target and platform.
- Preclinical and clinical pipeline.
- Registration, reimbursement, commercialization, and BD out-license.
- CDMO/CRO/service layer.
- Hospital and patient access.

Evidence to seek:

- Clinical phase, endpoint quality, safety, comparator, and population size.
- NDA/BLA/MAA status, approval timeline, guideline inclusion, and reimbursement.
- BD economics, upfront/milestone split, royalties, and partner strength.
- Cash runway, dilution risk, and commercial ramp.

Common downgrade patterns:

- Early pipeline priced like late-stage asset.
- Revenue gap hidden by one-time BD payment.
- Strong science with weak cash runway or regulatory visibility.

## Grid, Power Equipment, And Energy Infrastructure

Value-chain layers:

- UHV, transformer, switchgear, relay protection, automation, and digital grid.
- Inverter, PCS, energy storage, thermal management, and battery integration.
- Power semiconductor, magnetics, copper/aluminum, and key materials.
- EPC, operation, maintenance, and grid connection.
- End demand from renewables, data centers, industrial electrification, and policy capex.

Evidence to seek:

- State Grid / Southern Grid tender result, contract amount, delivery schedule, and share.
- Overseas certification, order backlog, price/margin trend, and receivables.
- Capex visibility, grid connection bottlenecks, and power-demand drivers.
- Policy timing and procurement cadence.

Common downgrade patterns:

- Policy narrative without tender or backlog evidence.
- Revenue growth without cash conversion.
- Equipment exposure with commodity-like margin pressure.

## Usage

1. Build or read a theme candidate universe before fetching individual candidates.
2. Map each candidate to one primary value-chain layer and one optional secondary layer.
3. Use the pack-specific evidence list to write AI overlay research questions.
4. Preserve hot directions as downgraded directions when direct evidence is missing.
5. Feed the final comparison report into Laplace when the user asks for strategy, allocation, or trend judgment.
